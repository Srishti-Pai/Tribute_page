Mars Orbiter Mission


Following is the chronology of events that traces the journey of Mangalyaan which lasted over 300 days:


  • 5th Nov 2013:ISRO’s PSLV C25 launches India’s Mars Orbiter Mission from Sriharikota, Andhra Pradesh.
  • 7th Nov :First Earth-bound manoeuvre performed.
  • 8th Nov :Second Earth-bound manoeuvre performed.
  • 9th Nov :Third Earth-bound manoeuvre performed.
  • 11th Nov :Fourth Earth-bound manoeuvre performed.
  • 12th Nov :Fifth Earth-bound manoeuvre performed.
  • 16th Nov :Sixth Earth-bound manoeuvre performed.
  • 1st Dec :MOM leaves Earth’s orbit, Trans-Mars Injection performed.

  • 4th Dec :MOM leaves Earth’s Sphere of Influence of 9.25 lakh km radius.
  • 11th Dec :First course correction manoeuvre performed on the spacecraft.
  • 11th June 2014:Second course correction manoeuvre executed.
  • 22nd Sep :MOM enters Mars’ Gravitational Sphere of Influence; 440 Newton Liquid Apogee Motor test-fired after over 300 days of dormancy; last trajectory correction manoeuvre performed.
  • 24th Sep :MOM reaches the intended orbit around Mars, making India the first country in the world to have successfully launched its mission to the Red Planet on the very first attempt.
